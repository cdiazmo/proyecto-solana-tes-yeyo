Manufactured piping spools for an EVEN TRAIN are valid to be located in an ODD 
TRAIN if minor works are performed on site. 
 
 
Molten salts pipes and accessories are located in the vertical plane, therefore 
completely interchangeable despite the train configuration used. No actions needed. 
 
HTF pipes and accessories need on site relocation to convert manufactured even train 
spools into an odd number system. 
 
 
In both cases, HEX-A is the cold unit while HEX-F is the hot unit.

Manufactured HTF pipes (B-to-C and D-to-E) are formed using three spools: 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
SPOOL #1 can be rotated so it can be welded to HEX-C/E. 
 
When doing so, drainage will not be in the lower point. There are two ways to fix this 
issue: 
 
1. Plug existing nozzle and open a new drainage point in the lower point. 
 
2. Sub-divide (cutting) the spool into an elbow+pipe and place the pipe in a way 
that the drainage point is located at the bot tom. This requires an additional 
field-weld. 
 
 
 
 
 
 

[page 3]
SPOOL #3 follows the same concept as the one above. 
 
In this case, the misplaced nozzle would be the vent. Same two options are suggested 
to point the vent nozzle upwards. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
SPOOL #2 only needs rotation. 
 
Due to this movement, PT nozzles will be directed inwards.
