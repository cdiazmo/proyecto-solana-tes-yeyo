1. Project Summary 
 
1.1 The Project is a concentrated solar power elect ric generating facility to be constructed on an 
approximately 1835-acre parcel of an existing 3000 acre tract in Maricopa County, Arizona, 
approximately 80 miles southwest of Phoenix, and 20 miles west of the town of Gila Bend near 
Interstate 8 and Painted Rock Dam Road. 
 
The Project is a 280 MW (gross) parabolic trough solar power plant with a net nominal electrical 
output of 250 MW, and all necessary supporting infrastructure, which make a fully operational 
facility. 
 
The facility design includes a thermal energy storage system will provide up to six hours of solar 
dispatchability. The solar field is oversized by about 70% relative to the actual calculated solar 
thermal requirements to ensure the plant will achieve summer on-peak capacity factors of 
approximately 100% from solar energy alone. 
 
The Project design will incorporate parabolic trough technology to capture solar energy in a heat 
transfer fluid (HTF). Steam will be generated in heat exchangers, i.e., solar steam generators 
(SSGs). The steam produced in the SSGs will then be sent to two steam turbines which will 
produce electrical power which will be sold to Arizona Public Service (APS). When the solar field 
collects more energy than is needed to satisfy the requirements of the Power Purchase 
Agreement (PPA) with the utility, the design of the HTF system shall incorporate the means for 
the excess solar energy collected by the HTF system to be directed to the Thermal Energy System 
(TES), where it will transfer its energy to molten nitrate salt during daytime hours with cloud 
cover or during night hours.
