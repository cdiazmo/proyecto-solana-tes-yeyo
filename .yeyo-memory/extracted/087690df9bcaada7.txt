Overview
In the present document, the TES exchanger foundations of Solana Generating Station are designed.
Description
Foundation’s shape and dimensions is shown in the next figure: 
The TES exchangers foundation are composed by two reinforced concrete footings, whose area is 49’2”x 11’6” and thickness of 3 feet, with three reinforced concrete walls per footing whose dimensions are 14’7” of height and 3’0” feet of thickness. These footings are projected to support the TES Exchangers Equipment. These footings are buried down 5 feet of engineered fill according to design instructions.

Design basis
For design purpose a static finite elements analysis have been performed using a beam elements frame in place of the foundation in order to apply the wind and earthquake loads.
Materials
In accordance with the Geotechnical Report, the soil has a sulfate concentration within the range of 44 to 170 p.p.m., therefore the exposure must be classified as S1. Though the soil water contains chlorides in concentrations of 120 to 430 ppm, the water level is more than ten metres below grade which makes this chloride contents irrelevant. Hence the following materials are chosen:

Design process:
Rebar quantity has been obtained according to ACI 318 specifications using the rectangular diagram method. Adherence, cracking and shear strength have been checked.
The footing has been reinforced with a minimum of 1.8 ‰ per each side as the ACI-318 indicates in 7.12.2.1 for mat foundations and this same reinforcement has been used as base reinforcement. Vertical reinforcement:
