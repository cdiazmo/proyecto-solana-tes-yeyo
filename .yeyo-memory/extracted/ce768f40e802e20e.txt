1. INTRODUCTION AND OBJECTIVE OF THE STUDY 
ABENGOA ENERGÍA, S.A., hereinafter ABENGOA, requests TECNALIA to carry out a corrosion study on 
several A516Gr70 carbon steel coupons , tested under preheating conditions, in a molten nitrate 
tank. 
 
Four parallelepiped coupons, references 17, 18, 19 and 20, and one U -bend stress corrosion cracking 
specimen, reference 9, are sent by ABENGOA to Tecnalia in order to carry out the following studies: 
 
- Examination of the A516Gr70 (hereinafter A516) coupons on receipt 
- Estimation of the general ized corrosion rate of the A516 coupons, according ASTM G1 
standard1 
- Metallographic study performed by optical and Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) 
- Chemical characterization of the corrosion products and deposits on the A516 coupons by 
Energy-Dispersive X-ray (EDX) and X-Ray Diffraction (XRD) spectroscopies 
- The cracking susceptibility of the U-bend specimen will be examined using the SEM 
 
The identification of the coupons, the size and the weight of the specimens before exposure, has 
been given by ABENGOA. According ABENGOA, the coupons have been tested since 27 may 2013, to 
23 June 2017, at 380 ºC, with operating periods in the range of 280 ºC, due to system shutdowns. 
 
The results obtained from the above mentioned studies are described in this document.

2. VISUAL EXAMINATION 
Macrographs in Figures 2.1 to 2.4 show the overall appearance of the preheated coupons. Except for 
the U-bend coupon, the corrosion morphology and extension of the coupons is quite similar. 
 
The surface of the A516 coupons shows abundant white to orange salt deposits. Under these 
deposits, a grey -blackish corrosion layer, apparently well adhered in almost the entire coupon 
surface, is observed. Uniform general corrosion appears to be the predominant mechanism 
observed. Pitting or other kind of localized corrosion is not detected. 
 
Few small areas with reddish brown corrosion products are observed on A516 coupon ref. 18 (Figure 
2.3). These areas will be analyzed by SEM microscopy in more detail.

3. GENERAL CORROSION RATE ESTIMATION 
The general corrosion rate of A516 coupons is estimated following the indications of ASTM G1 
standard. According to this standard, the “average” corrosion rate is calculated using the formula: 
 
Corrosion rate = (K x p) / A x t x D 
 
Where: K = Constant K in the corrosion rate equation is 8.76 x 107, if the corrosion rate units are 
 expressed in microns per year (μm/year) 
 p = Initial weight (before exposure) – weight of descaled test specimen (g) 
 t = Test duration (hours) 
 A = Test specimen area (cm2) 
 D = Density of metal specimen (g/cm3) 
 
The removal of the corrosion products in the carbon steel coupons is carried out with the solution 
reference C.3.5 (Table A1.1 in ASTM G1 standard): Hydrochloric acid + hexamethylene tetramine. 
 
The corrosion rate values obtained for corrosion coupons ref. 17, 18 and 19, are given in Table 1. 
 
Some considerations: 
- A516 carbon steel density is 7.86 g/cm3 
- Coupons have been tested from 27 May 2013, to 23 June 2017, equivalent to around 35720 
hours of testing . According ABENGOA , due to system shutdowns , the total exposure time 
should be reduced by approximately one hal f. This gives a total time exposure of 17860 
hours. This is the period of time introduced in the corrosion rate formula. 
- The initial total surface of the specimen is determined making corrections for the area 
associated with the mounting hole. Initial dimensions 25.5 x 51 x 1.8 (mm), hole diameter 10 
mm.

4. METALLOGRAPHIC STUDY 
A cross s ection extracted from A516 coupon ref. 20 is prepared for metallographic study , by 
embedding this cross section in resin and polishing. Th is metallographic probe is further studied by 
optical and SEM microscopy , in order to study the morphology and extension of the corrosion 
damage. 
 
The o ptical micrographs shown in Figure 4.1 reveal a relatively uniform oxide layer, in terms of 
thickness, and in general firmly attached to the surface of the coupon. No pitting or other kind of 
localized corrosion is seen.

5. SEM/EDX AND DRX ANALYSES OF CORROSION PRODUCTS AND DEPOSITS 
In order to chemically characteri ze the corrosion products and deposits generated in the A516 
coupons, EDX analyses are carried out using a microanalyse r coupled to a scanning electron 
microscope. 
 
Table 2 lists the semi -quantitative elemental chemical composition obtained in the ED X analyses 
performed on the surface of coupon ref. 20 (before cutting and embedding in resin). These analyses 
show a significant amount of magnesium (Mg) and oxygen (O), practically on the entire surface of the 
coupon. EDX analyses of the reddish corrosion spots show them to be enriched with iron (Fe). 
 
The detected magnesium oxide takes the form of a very crystalline deposit, as it can be seen in 
Figure 5.1.

The metallographic cross section extracted from A516 coupon ref. 20, is also analysed by SEM/EDX 
microscopy, in order to obtain more information about the composition of the corrosion layer . EDX 
analyses are conducted at different locations of the oxide scale thickness. Figure 5.2 shows a back -

The obtained results indicate that magnesium oxide deposition takes place in the outermost part of 
the iron oxide layer. Magnesium is a minor constituent of the nitrate salt.

In order to stoichiometrically characterise the corrosion products and deposits generated on the 
surface of A516 coupon ref. 20, X-ray diffraction analyses are carried out using a Brucker D8-Advance 
X-ray-Diffractometer. The spectr um obtained and the identification of the main peaks is shown in 
Figure 5.2. The most significant results are detailed below: 
 
- The white to orange magnesium deposit is mainly constituted of periclase (MgO) 
- The iron oxide layer generated could correspond to magnetite (Fe3O4) 
- Although the DRX database indicates the likelihood for some peaks to correspond to sodium 
oxide (NaO), sodium has not been detected in the EDX analyses, so, if present, it should not 
exceed 0,4 - 0,5 percent by weight. This amount is also well below the detection limit 
specified for the DRX equipment. Thus, it is not possible to determine the presence of NaO 
by DRX, either because there is not NaO, or, if present, it is at a very low concentration 
- It is important to point out that in the XRD analyses only the crystalline species (diffracting 
ones) are shown. The sharp and high intensity peak of the magnesium oxide is due to its high 
crystalline nature

6. U-BEND COUPON EXAMINATION 
Corrosion products in the U-bend coupon are partially removed with a plastic brush. The surface is 
then examined by SEM. No cracking is observed at x 500 magnification. 
 
 
 
7. CONCLUSIONS 
 A516Gr70 carbon steel coupons tested under preheating conditions undergo generalized 
corrosion. Pitting or other kind of localized corrosion is not detected 
 
 The a verage generalised corrosion rate measured for A516 coupons is about 1.66 ± 0 .12 
microns/year, for a testing period of 17860 hours. This corrosion rate value is the ave rage of 
3 descaled test coupons 
 
 A516Gr70 coupons form protective iron oxide films in the molten salt media. The XRD 
analysis identifies iron oxide as magnetite (Fe3O4) 
 
 Deposits on tested coupons are principally constituted of magnesium oxide (MgO) 
 
 Cracking is not observed in the U-bend coupon
