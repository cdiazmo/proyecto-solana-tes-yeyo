1.0 OBJECT 
 
This procedure describes the met hod and requirements for visible dye 
penetrant examination using the solvent removable process. 
This procedure is applicable for the detection of discontinuities that are open to 
the surface in component s of non-porous metallic material, welds and 
temporary attachments. 
 
 
2.0 SCOPE 
 
As specified in the particular Inspection Program of the component. 
 
 
3.0 CODES 
 
ASME Code, Section V – Non Destructive Examination (2015 Edition). 
ASME Code, Section VIII Div 1 y 2 – Pressure Vessels (2015 Edition). 
ASME Section I, Power Boilers (2015 Edition). 
ASME B 31.1 Power Piping (2012 Edition) 
WRITTEN PRACTICE ASME. QCM.PR05 
SNT-TC-1A (2006 Edition) 
 
4.0 RESPONSIBILITIES AND AUTHORITY 
 
4.1.0 QUALITY MANAGER 
 
He has the authority to determine the it ems that must be checked using this 
procedure. 
 
 
4.2.0 QUALIFIED PERSONNEL 
 
The personnel who will perform the liqui d penetrant examinations must be 
qualified according to Lointek S.L. Written Practice ASME.QCM.PR05. In 
those cases where the tests are required to meet the EC Pressure Equipment 
Directive, personnel must be qua lified as per ISO 9712/2012 (EN-473) 
specifies. 
 
It will also be valid that the subcontract or will be certified according to their 
own written practice. If the subcontractor 's written practice is approved by the 
QC Manager. 
 
The results must be interpreted by a Level II or III qualified and certified 
personnel.

5.0 PRODUCTS 
Preferably the products to use will be the following ones: 
 
 Dye Penetrant ARDROX 996PB 
 
 Remover ARDROX 9 PR5 
 
 Developer ARDROX 9 D1B 
 
Another similar product coul d be used with the approval of the Quality Control 
Manager and they will be demonstrated to the A.I. 
 
The products must be compatible with the material to examine and with the 
final use of the examined piece. It is not allowed to mix different makers' 
products. 
 
The products used for the examination in Nickel base alloys, austenitic or 
duplex stainless steel and titanium must be according with Appendix II Article 
6 Section V of the ASME CODE. 
 
 
6.0 SURFACE CONDITION 
 
6.1.0. CLEANING 
The areas to be examined and all adjacent areas within at least 1 inch (25 
mm.) will be clean (free of dust, oxide, welding splatter, oils, machining marks 
or any other that can in terfere with the exam or the interpretation) and will be 
dry before applying the penetrant. 
The cleaning will be made applying solvent: ARDROX 9PR5. 
Drying after cleaning of the surface to be examined shall be accomplished by 
normal evaporation or with forced hot or cold air. After all visible traces of the 
cleaner have been removed, a minimum of five minutes drying time shall be 
established prior to application of the penetrant. 
 
6.2.0 TEMPERATURE 
 
The temperature of t he penetrant and the examined surface will be between 
the 5ºC and 52ºC throughout t he examination period. Local heating or cooling 
is permitted. 
 
6.3.0 LIGHTING 
For visible penetrant, the minimum light intensity will be 1000 lux. The light 
intensity shall be measured with light meter prior to the evaluation of 
indications.

7.0 DESCRIPTION OF THE PROCESS 
 
7.1.0 APPLICATION OF THE PENETRANT 
The liquid penetrant examination sha ll be performed in accordance with 
minimum and maximum listed in Table 1 (attached) 
 
The examined area must be fully cover ed with the penetrant. This application 
will be done using the following methods: dipping, brushing, or spraying. 
 
 
7.2.0 PENETRANT DWELL TIME 
The appropriate time of penetration for t he range of temperatures indicated on 
the paragraph 6.2.0 of this procedure is at least of 15 minutes. 
 
During this period the penetrant liquid s hall not be allowed to dry, and shall be 
applied several times if required. 
 
For temperatures from 5ºC to 10ºC, minimum time shall be 20 minutes. 
 
The maximum penetrant dwell time shall not exceed the maximum 
recommended by the manufacture or 2 hours. 
 
 
7.3.0 EXCESS PENETRANT REMOVAL 
The removal method depends on the product that is used. 
 
- Organic solvent 
 
First the excess penetrant shall be remo ved by wiping with cloth or absorbent 
paper, repeating the operat ion until most traces of penetrant have been 
removed. Later the cleaning process continues with rags moistened with 
solvent until the examined area and the adjacent ones will be fully cleaned. 
 
It is not allowed to apply the solvent directly on the examined area during the 
cleaning previous to the developer application. 
 
 
7.4.0 DRYING AFTER EXCESS PENETRANT REMOVAL 
The examined surfaces must be totally dry, before applying the developer. 
 
The surface may be dried by normal evaporation, blotting, wiping or forced air.

Drying time after removal of excess penet rant and prior to the application of 
the developer shall be held to a minimu m of 5 minutes and a maximum of 30 
minutes. 
 
 
7.5.0 DEVELOP ING 
The developer shall be applied within 5 minutes after penetrant removal. 
The developer shall be applied immediately after excess penetrant removal. 
Prior to applying wet developer to the surface, the developer spray must be 
thoroughly agitated to ensure adequate mixture. 
 
 
The developer shall be applied on the examined surface getting a fine layer free of 
local accumulations. 
The developer shall be applied within 15 minutes after penetrant removal. 
 
 
7.6.0. INTERPRETATION 
Developing time for final interpreta tion begins as soon as wet developer 
coating is dry. 
 
Final interpretation shall be made not less than 10 minutes, nor more than 60 
minutes. 
If the surface to be examined is large enough to preclude complete 
examination, the examination shall be performed in increments. 
 
The surface must be observed during the application of the developer to follow 
the behaviour of the indications and during the test. 
 
 
7.7.0 POST-CLEANING 
Post cleaning is necessary when resi dual products could interfere with 
subsequent processing or with service requirements. 
 
When post cleaning is requir ed, it should be conducted as soon as practical 
after evaluation and documentation with approved products.

8.0 EVALUATION 
 
An indication is the evidence of a mechanical discontinuity. 
An indication of an imperfection may be larger than the imperfection that 
causes it; however, the size of the indication is the basis for acceptance 
evaluation. Only indications with major dimensions greater than 1,5 mm shall 
be considered relevant. 
 
 Linear indications are those having a length exceeding three times the 
width of the indication. 
 
 Rounded indications are those which ar e circular or elliptical and have a 
length of less than three times the width of the indication. 
 
 Any doubtful or questionable indication will be checked again to verify if 
it is relevant. 
 
Any indication that is believed to be non-relevant shall be regarded as a defect 
until the indication is elim inated by surface conditioning or evaluated by other 
non-destructive means and demonstrated to be non-relevant. 
 
When two adjacent indications are s eparated by a lower distance than the 
bigger dimension of the smallest one, they should be evaluated as a single 
continuous indication. 
 
The final evaluation of the dimension of the indication should be carried out 
after the minimum time of development has lapsed and before the indication 
has decayed until the point of not representing the origin of the imperfection.
