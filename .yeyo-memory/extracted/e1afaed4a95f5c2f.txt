Notes:
1. Lap joint flanges and stub ends are to be used only as required to assure proper bolt-hole alignment at odd-angle piping configurations.
2. When flanged gate valves 2” and smaller are required use FA84.
3. FC85 is to be used only in the boiler feedwater line at the inlet to the waste hea t boiler as specified on the flow diagrams and at no other location.
4. All piping shall comply with ANSI B31.3 basic code (fabrication and erection, Section 327; inspection and examination, Section 340 and 341) unless otherwise 
specified in the shop fabrication specification.
5. Sockolets or weldolets are preferred for use at tie-in locations regardless of size change due to difficulty of realignment of piping after cutting.
6. Upon prolonged exposure to temperature above 800  F., the carbide phase of carbon steel may be converted to graphite (ASME B31.3, 2008, TABLE A-1, 
NOTE 57). Values indicated in pressure / temperature chart (page 2 of 2) up to 750  F. are per ASME B31.3, 2008, Table A-1 and could be de-rated to be used 
over 750 F with extreme caution on a case by case basis, but in no case exceed the 800  F. limit
7. All pipe & fittings to be internally cleaned per spec 4521-ESP-ANC-00-61-PS010.
8. At a minimum, all shop and field root pass welds shall be done using Tungsten Inert Gas ( “TIG” – “Heliarc”) welding process.
9. All pipe shall be installed “clean”. The minimum standard shall be free of mill scale, slag, dirt, construction debris and foreign objects.
