1. Aim 
 
The purpose of this document is to define the Operational limits of the Oil to Salt 
Heat Exchanger of Solana Thermosolar Power Plant (Arizona, USA). 
2. Design condition 
Oil side (tube side): 
 Design 
Temperature 399 ºC 750.2 ºF 
Pressure 22 & FV barg 320 & FV psig 
 
 
Salt side (shell side): 
 Design 
Temperature 399 ºC 750.2 ºF 
Pressure 22 & FV barg 320 & FV psig 
3. Variation ratio in operation conditions 
Once the Oil to Salt Heat Exchangers is set in operation, the following are the 
maximum allowable temperature variations allowed: 
 
• Thermal shock +/- 126 ºF (70 ºC), i.e. rapid temperature change for incoming 
fluids must not be greater than +/- 126 ºF (70 ºC). 
This thermal shock is measured at the inlet of both sides, considering the 
following: 
- Thermal shock HTF side = Temperature from Solar Field/SGS – 
Temperature at the inlet of the heat exchangers. 
- Thermal shock Salt side = Temperature from hot/cold salt tank – 
Temperature at the inlet of the heat exchangers.

• Maximum heating/cooling ramp for incoming fluids must not be greater than 
18 ºF/min (10 ºC/min). 
4. Operation area 
 
A. Temperatures 
 
There are three different situations to set the equipment into operation: 
• Initially there is no flow of two fluids 
When one of the fluids starts to flow, its inlet temperature must not be either 
higher or lower than 126 ºF (70 ºC) over the temperature of that fluid inside 
the train. 
 
|ΔT| < 126 ºF 
 
 
 
 
 
 
In the event of prolonged stand-by of the equipment and in order not to 
surpass the above limit, the maximum molten salts flow to be run through the 
equipment shall not exceed the 5% of the design flow.

The temperatures of the equipment will be measured with the instruments located 
upstream considering the operation mode they perform under (Charge or 
Discharge). 
 
Heat exchanger has always to be filled with salt to avoid a great temperature 
difference between shell and tube side before starting the operation. 
 
From the temperature limitation point of view, which fluid (cold or hot) begins to 
circulate has no influence. 
 
B. Mass flow 
 
It is considered that there is HTF flow when the value is higher than 435.600 lb/hr 
(55 kg/s) per train. 
 
It is considered that there is SALT flow when the value is higher than 674.600 lb/hr 
(85 kg/s) per train. 
 
Maximum SALT mass flow allowed of the equipment is 7.284.705 lb/hr (920 kg/s) 
per train. 
 
Maximum HTF mass flow allowed of the equipment is 4.516.173 lb/hr (570 kg/s) per 
train. 
5. Others 
 
There is no special temperature limitation for the drain with N2, apart for the 18 
ºF/min (10 ºC/min) temperature ramp. In this case, the ramp will be measured with 
the average of the drain outlet temperatures.

The above mentioned limitations (sections 3, 4, and 5) will also apply for

There is no limitation for pressure differences between fluids.
