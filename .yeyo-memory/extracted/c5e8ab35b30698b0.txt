4. - ORGANIZATION 
 
LOINTEK, S.L. planned the following organization for carrying out this order: 
See Document :MC-SECC 04 Organization Chart (attached) 
Each Manager may delegate the performance of any of his duties to personnel 
immediately under his authority. It is emphasized howe ver, that the responsibility of 
these duties cannot be delegated and remains with the Manager himself. 
A Manager may perform the duties of personnel under his responsibility, if he is 
qualified for the duties. 
The following clauses explain the duties of the people involved with the Quality 
Management System. 
 
General Manager 
The General Manager has overall responsibility for managing all the activities carried 
out at LOINTEK, S.L. 
 
Commercial Manager 
The Commercial Manager has overall responsibility for: 
- Preparing the quotations for the new projects. 
- Contacting with the customer for the contract development. 
- Marketing activities. 
- Receiving the orders from the customers. 
 
Purchasing Manager 
The Purchasing Manager has overall responsibility for: 
- Marketing research and analysis in order to ascertain the availability of the 
material strictly in compliance with Code requirements and with company 
scheduling besides the economical requirements. 
- Preparation of the Purchase Orders. 
- Distribution of Purchase Orders. 
 
Industrial Manager 
The Industrial Manager has overall responsibility for managing all the manufacturing 
activities carried out at LOINTEK, S.L. These activities include the supervision of all 
the departments involved with Mechanical Engineering, Plann ing, Manufacturing and 
Welding of Code Items. 
 
Engineering Manager 
The Engineering Manager has overall responsibility for: 
- Development of drawings and calculations.

Production Manager 
The Production Manager has overall responsibility for:

Welding Engineer 
The Welding Engineer has overall responsibility for 
- Preparing and approving of the Welding Procedure Specifications (WPS). 
- Conducting the qualification tests and reporting them on Procedure 
Qualification Records (PQR). He certifies the PQRs. 
- Managing the performance qualifications of Welders and Welding Operat ors 
and documenting and certifying them on WPQ's/WOPQ’s. 
- Maintaining the Welders log up-to date. 
- Assignment of welder’s identification symbols. 
 
Project Manager 
The Project Manager is responsible for: 
 -Coordinate the different departments involves in this project. 
 -Planning actualization. 
 -Communications with the customer. 
 
Workshop Manager 
The Workshop Manager has overall responsibility for 
- Instructing and assigning qualified Welders and Welding Operators to Code 
jobs. 
 
Quality Manager 
The Quality Manager reports directly to the General Manager and has the following 
functions: 
- Preparation, approval, distribution, implementation, revision and control of the 
Quality Manual.
