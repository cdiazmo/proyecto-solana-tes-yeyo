Notes:
1. Normal wall this spec to be used above 250 PSIG through 285 PSIG. Minimum wall this spec to be used only through 250 PSIG 
maximum.
2. Screwed connections to be made with TFE paste. Paste to be “Bakerseal” #899-21, Baker Oil Tools, Inc., Locktite #59231 or equal 
(furnished by field).
3. On 3” through 24” use weld neck flanges on buttweld fittings except lap joint flanges and stub ends are to be used only as required to 
assure proper bolt-hole alignment at odd-angle piping configurations.
4. Where flanged gate valves 2” and smaller are required, use FA44.
5. Process gas is intended to be the piping from carbon steel duct work to sulfur burner sight ports, etc.
6. All piping shall comply with ANSI B31.3 basic code (fabrication and erection, Section 327; inspection and examination, Section 340 and 
341) except when system pressure is less than 150 PSI and temperature is less th an 366 F. ANSI B31.3 Category “D” shall apply.
7. FV40Z knife gate valve used for tie-ins only. In normal operations valve remains open.
8. Slip-on flanges above 4 ” size are not allowed in ASME boiler “code” piping (see ANSI / ASME B31.1, Section 122.1.1, par agraph “F”, 
edition 1989). Use weld neck flanges as required.
9. Sockolets or weldolets are preferred for use at tie -in locations regardless of size change due to difficulty of realignment of piping after 
cutting.
10.Specification limited to 500  F. Max. to av oid concern about temperature cycling of slip -on flange joint (B31.3, Section 308.2.1). For 
higher temperature requirements see Specification PS -CS71.
11.All pipe & fittings to be internally cleaned per spec 4521-ESP-ANC-00-61-PS010.
12.At a minimum, all shop and field root pass welds shall be done using Tungsten Inert Gas ( “TIG” – “Heliarc”) welding process.
13.All pipe shall be installed “clean”. The minimum standard shall be free of mill scale, slag, dirt, construction debris and foreign objects.
