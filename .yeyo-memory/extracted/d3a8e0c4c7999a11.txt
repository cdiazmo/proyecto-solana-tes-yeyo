Part 1 - General 
 
1.1 Summary 
 
1.1.1 Specification Includes: 
 
(1) Excavation for building foundations, slabs-on-grade, paving, and site structures. 
 
(2) Trenching for underground pipe, duct, and conduit. 
 
(3) Site filling to establish subgrade contours. 
 
(4) Backfilling building perimeter and site structures to subgrade elevations. 
 
(5) Fill under slabs-on-grade and paving. 
 
(6) Fill for over-excavation. 
 
(7) Compaction. 
 
(8) Landscape grading and topsoil placement. 
 
1.1.2 Related Specifications 
 
(1) Deleted 
 
(2) Specification 4251-ESP-ANC-00-50-2230 – Site Clearing and Rough Grading 
 
(3) Specification 4251-ESP- ANC -00-50-2342 – Soil Stabilization 
 
(4) Specification 4251-ESP- ANC -00-50-2721 – Aggregate Base Course

3.3 Excavation 
 
3.3.1 Take special precautions as required preserving con dition and integrity of existing 
structures. Special precautions would include sheeting, shoring, bracing, etc. 
 
3.3.2 Excavate subsoil required to accommodate building f oundations, slabs-on grade, paving, 
and site structures. 
 
3.3.3 Scarify the exposed soil to an additional depth of 6 inches; adjust the soil moisture by 
adding water or dryer so the soil moisture content is within 3% of the optimum 
moisture content. 
 
3.3.4 Compact the subgrade soils to at least 98% Standard Proctor maximum dry density in 
accordance with ASTM D698. 
 
3.3.5 Grade top perimeter of excavation to prevent surfac e water from draining into 
excavation. 
 
3.3.6 Use precaution during final excavation to subgrade level to prevent disturbance and 
remolding of subgrade material. Hand trim excavati on as required. Remove loose 
material. 
 
3.3.7 Remove lumped subsoil, boulders, and rock up; to 1/3 cubic yard measured by volume. 
 
3.3.8 Notify the Engineer’s Field Representative of unexp ected subsurface conditions or 
hazardous materials encountered. Discontinue affec ted work in area until notified to 
resume work. 
 
3.3.9 It is the Contractor’s responsibility to comply wit h applicable State and Federal 
regulations on excavation, shoring and trenching.

3.5 Filling And Backfilling 
 
3.5.1 Fill areas to contours and elevations with unfrozen materials. Do not backfill over 
porous, wet, frozen, or spongy subgrade surfaces. 
 
3.5.2 Cut out soft areas of subgrade not capable of in si tu compaction. Backfill with Type A 
fill or Type C fill, as appropriate, and compact to 98% Standard Proctor density. 
 
3.5.3 Place fill materials in continuous layers not exceeding 8-inch loose depth and compact to 
98% Standard Proctor density (ASTM D698). 
 
3.5.4 Install and compact a 6-inch thickness of well-grad ed crushed stone with maximum 6% 
fines beneath building slabs. 
 
3.5.5 Inaccessible areas should be compacted using hand equipment in 4-inch loose lifts. 
 
3.5.6 Maintain optimum moisture content (± 3%) of all fil l materials to attain required 
compaction density. 
 
3.5.7 If subgrade material or previously placed subsoil f ill has deteriorated due to weather 
exposure, scarify the top 2 inches of material to e stablish an acceptable interface prior 
to placing any additional fill.

3.6 Placing Topsoil

subgrade. 
 
3.6.4 Remove roots, weeds, rocks, and foreign material while spreading. 
 
3.6.5 Manually spread topsoil close to trees, plants, and buildings to prevent damage. 
 
3.6.6 Lightly compact placed topsoil. Grade topsoil to e liminate rough, low or soft areas, and 
to ensure positive drainage away from structures. 
 
3.6.7 Remove surplus subsoil and topsoil from site.
