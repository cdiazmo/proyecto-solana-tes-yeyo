Purpose and scope
This document is intended to evaluate the implications of replacing the existing TES System plate heat exchangers, for a new set of heat exchangers of the tube and shell.
The new proposed heat exchangers will have different pressure drops and will be located at a different location and at heights of the existing ones. To evaluate the consequences of installing this equipment on the existing HTF and Molten Salts circuits, a set of hydraulic calculations were simulated using Pipeflo ®, for both systems and different operating cases. Molten Salts Circuit
System description
The plant includes 6 identical and independent molten salts circuits. For simulation purposes the following equipment where considered:
One (1) hot salts tank (1/../6 -MT-252)
One (2) cold salts tank (1/../6 -MT-251)
One (1) HTF-to-Salt Heat Exchanger (1/../6 -MX-251)
One (1) hot salts pump (1/../6 -MP-252)
One (1) cold salts pump (1/../6 -MP-251)
Because there are no differences in terms of geometry and equipment performance, the molten salts circuit was simulated for a single set of tanks for the different operating modes.

HTF Circuit
System 
The HTF circuit is a closed circuit where its backpressure is ensured by the expansion tank.
In terms of equipment includes 10 identical main pumps (2 of which in standby) that send the colds HTF to the solar field and through 6 identical HTF-to-Salts heat exchangers and two steam generators.
For simulation purposes the following equipment where considered:
Ten (10) HTF Far Field Pumps (MP-200 A/B/C/D/E/F/G/H/J/K)
Twelve (12) pressure regulating valves at the HTF pumps discharge (FV20005 A/B/C/D/E/F/G/H/J/K)
Six (6) HTF-to-Salt Heat Exchanger (1/../6 -MX-251)
Six (6) HTF-to-Salt Heat Exchanger Filters (1/../6 -MF-251C)
Six (6) HTF-to-Salt Heat Exchanger Filters (1/../6 -MF-251D)
Six (6) HTF-to-Salt Heat Exchanger Flow Regulating Valves (1/../6 FV25135)
Six (6) HTF-to-Salt Heat Exchanger Flow Regulating Valves (1/../6 FV2513)
