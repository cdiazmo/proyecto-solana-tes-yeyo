The object of the present procedure is to establish the necessary and sufficient 
conditions for visual examination of weld seams and base materials. 
 
 
2.0 SCOPE

mating surfaces, shape, and all welding join ts the same to butt welds than fillet 
welds.

5.0 GENERAL REQUIREMENTS 
 
1. The Inspector performing visual weld ex aminations shall verify that the item 
correlates with the paperwork and that a ll required previous manufacturing 
operations and inspections have been successfully completed and signed off. 
 
2. If any necessary stamping to identify ma terial or individual performing welding 
processes is missing, the Inspector will observe such stamping after validation 
by company records prior to acceptance. 
 
3. The Inspector will assure that the conditi on of the weld surface an at least 1" on 
either side of the weld to be exami ned are free of scale, paint, grease, or 
accumulation of dirt or excessive irregularities that would mask a deficiency. 
 
4. Visual examination personnel shall be examined annually to assure natural or 
corrected near distance acuity in at least one eye, such that they are capable of 
reading Jaeger No. 1 letters at a distanc e of not less than 12". Personnel shall 
also demonstrate the capability of dist inguishing and differentiating contrast 
between colors or shades of gray. 
 
5. Visual examinations shall be performed to assure correct weld size, length, and 
location.Measurements may be achieved by using devices such as rulers, 
templates, gauges, micrometers, im pressions, magnification such as 
boroscopes or magnifying glass or by comparison with standards such as 
workmanship samples or photographs, as necessary to evaluate specified 
physical attributes of welds and weldments prior to verifying the final inspection. 
 
6. Direct visual examinations 
 
Direct visual examinations shall be made when access is sufficient to place the 
eye within 600 mm of the surface to be examined and at an angle not less than 
30 degrees to the surface to be examined. Mirrors may be used to improve the 
angle of vision and aids such as a m agnifying lens may be used to assist 
examination. 
 
Illumination (Natural or supplemental white light) for the specific part, 
component, vessel or section thereof being examined is required. 
The minimum light level shall be 1000 lu x. The light intensity shall be 
measured/verified with a meter prior to the examination. 
 
Light meters shall be calibrated at leas t once a year or whenever the meter has 
been repaired. If meters have not been in us e for one year or more, calibration 
shall be done before being used. 
 
7. Remote Visual Examination 
 
In cases where the welds are not accessi ble for direct visual examination, 
remote visual examination may be substi tuted for direct examination. Remote 
visual examination may use visual aids such as mirror, telescopes, boroscopes, 
fiber optics, cameras, or other suitable instruments. Such systems shall have a 
resolution capability at least equivalent to that obtainable by direct visual 
observation.

6. Inspección visual directa. 
Las inspecciones visuales directas deben r ealizarse cuando el acceso es 
suficiente para situar el ojo a m enos de 600 mm de la superficie a 
examinar y un ángulo no menor de 30 º a la superficie a examinar. Se 
pueden usar espejos para mejorar el ángulo de visión y aparatos como 
lentes de aumento para ayudar en la inspección. 
 
Es necesaria la iluminación (natural o artificial con luz blanca) para la 
parte específica, componente, equipo o sección a examinar. El mínimo 
nivel de iluminación será 1000 lux en la superficie de ensayo. 
 
El luxómetro deberá ser calibrado como mí nimo una vez al año o cada vez que 
se repare. Si el equipo no ha sido us ado en un año o más, se deberá calibrar 
antes de su uso. 
 
7. Inspección visual remota. 
 
En los casos donde las soldaduras no est án accesibles para realizar una 
inspección directa, la inspección visual remota sustituirá a la directa. 
En la inspección visual remota se pueden usar aparatos como espejos, 
telescopios, boroscopios, fibras ópticas, cámaras u otros instrumentos 
apropiados. Esos sistemas deben tener una capacidad de resolución al menos 
equivalente a la resolución obtenida mediante inspección visual directa.
