4. - ORGANIZATION 
 
LOINTEK, S.L. planned the following organization for carrying out this order: 
 
See Document :MC-SECC 04 Organization Chart (attached) 
 
 
Each Manager may delegate the performance of any of his duties to personnel 
immediately under his authority. It is emphasized however, that the responsibility of these 
duties cannot be delegated and remains with the Manager himself. 
 
A Manager may perform the duties of personnel under his responsib ility, if he is qualified 
for the duties. 
 
The following clauses explain the duties of the people involved with the Quality 
Managment System. 
 
General Manager 
 
The General Manager has overall responsibility for managing all the activities carried out 
at LOINTEK, S.L. 
 
Commercial Manager 
 
The Comercial Manager has overall responsibility for 
 
- Preparing the quotations for the new projects. 
- Contacting with the customer for the contract development. 
- Marketing activities. 
- Receiving the orders from the customers. 
 
Purchasing Manager 
 
The Purchasing Manager has overall responsibility for 
 
- Marketing research and analysis in order to ascertain the availability of the material 
strictly in compliance with Code r equirements and with company scheduling 
besides the economical requirements. 
- Preparation of the Purchase Orders. 
- Distribution of Purchase Orders 
 
Industrial Manager 
 
The Industrial Manager has overall respons ibility for managing all the manufacturing 
activities carried out at LOINTEK, S.L. These activities include the supervision of all the 
departments involved with Mechanical Engineer ing, Planning, Manufacturing and Welding 
of Code Items.

Engineering Manager 
 
The Engineering Manager has overall responsibility for 
 
- Development of drawings and calculations. 
- Approval of calculations 
- Issuing and controlling drawings valid for fabrication. 
- Approval Material / Welding Material Purchase Requisition List. 
- Preparation of Manufacturer’s Data Report. 
 
Production Manager 
 
The Production Manager has overall responsibility for: 
 
- Scheduling, coordinating and performing of all activities concerning the shop works 
having under his direct authority in the plant. 
- Storage of materials involved in “ASME” construction. 
- Shipment of completed units or parts, and for all maintenance activities. 
 
Welding Engineer 
 
The Welding Engineer has overall responsibility for 
 
- Preparing and approving of the Welding Procedure Specifications (WPS). 
- Conducting the qualification tests and r eporting them on Proc edure Qualification 
Records (PQR). He certifies the PQRs 
- Managing the performance qualifications of Welders and Welding Operators and 
documenting and certifying them on WPQ's/WOPQ’s. 
- Maintaining the Welders log up-to date. 
- Assingment of welder’s identification symbols 
 
Project Manager 
 
The Project Manager is responsible for: 
 
 -Coordinate the different department s involves in this project. 
 -Planning actualization. 
 -Communications with the customer . 
 
Workshop Manager 
 
The Workshop Manager has overall responsibility for 
 
- Instructing and assigning qualified Welders and Welding Operators to Code jobs. 
 
Quality Manager 
 
The Quality Manager reports directly to the General Manager and has the following 
functions: 
 
- Preparation, approval, distri bution, implementation, re vision and control of the 
Quality Manual 
- Implementation of specific ations and procedures used to assure conformance of 
the product to the Code requirements. 
- Concurrence and approval of the resolution of the non-conformities. 
- Calibration of measuring and test equipment. 
- Review and approval of Material Test Reports.
