RE: Solana Salt Heat Exchanger 53’L x 9’ W x 11’6”H 715,840# (including saddle).
PREPHASE: Off load New Salt Heat Exchanger from rail in Gila Bend and transporting and off loading to cribbing at plant site
 If Company wants a bid for off loading at rail in Gila Bend, transporting, and off loading at laydown yard; Bragg Crane will be happy to quote that as a separate item if requested.
PHASE 1: Setting New Salt Heat Exchanger
• Ruby Engineering representative completes site visit, minimum three months prior to lift.
• Engineering & Drawings to be supplied for the Lift Plan. 
• Bragg Crane will set up gantry to pick and load New Heat Exchanger onto PHH 1 ½ wide Goldhofer. PHH moves vessel to staging area of cranes at plant site. Bragg Crane sets up a 450 ton Crawler Series 2 Heavy Lift and a 350 ton Crawler west of Old Salt Heat Exchanger. Crane mats will be placed per engineered drawing with the assistance of the appropriate assist cranes. Once the two crawlers are set up; Liberty Crane will complete an inspection of both cranes per regulations. 
• Complete training of personnel for plant. 
• PHH will move New Salt Heat Exchanger to final location on plant site for cranes to pick off of trailer. Once at final destination, Bragg Crane will rig onto vessel with the 450 ton crawler connecting to pick point on top end. The 350 ton crawler will then be connected to bottom of vessel . Once both cranes have had their rigging connected to the vessel, Bragg Crane Supervisor will direct lift. Both cranes will pick their end until vessel is off of trailer. Once controlled, the 450 ton crawler will begin to lift and the 350 ton crawler will lower at tail crane. Once the 450 ton crawler has vessel uprighted and rigging detached from 350 ton (without getting under load), the 450 ton crawler will walk the tank to the new pad and set in place. Rigging will be disconnected using a man lift with qualified operator and all personnel tied off. Cranes will move back and begin to dismantle and move out of plant. 
PHASE 2: Removing old Salt Heat Exchanger
When the plant designates that New Salt Heat Exchanger is functioning properly; PHH and Bragg Crane will move onto site in preparation for removing Old Salt Heat Exchanger. PHH will set up Trailer for transporting the vessel once it is set onto trailer. Bragg crane will move in (1) 650 ton crawler and (1) 450 ton crawler. Once cranes are set up they will move the 650 ton to the south of the new Heat Exchanger, considering 125 ‘ radius, approximately 275’ boom, hook height of 75’. The 450 ton will move to north of the new pad, considering 60’ radius, approximately 120’ boom, and hook height of 75’. The 650 ton will hook up to the east side and the 450 ton will hook up to the west side. They will be directed by the Supervisor to lift equally and keep tank parallel to the ground and bring it as close to them as is safe. Once in that position they will both crawl back out of the hole until clear enough for the PHH trailer to get into place. The tank will then be set onto the PHH trailer. Once the rigging is disconnected and the trailer has moved out, the crawlers will move back into place to dismantle and exit site. Bragg Crane will use a Gantry to off load the Old Salt Heat Exchanger in bone yard and set onto cribbing.
