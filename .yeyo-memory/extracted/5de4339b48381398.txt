1.0 OBJECT 
 
This procedure describes t he method and requirements f or visible dye 
penetrant examination using the solvent removable process. 
This procedure is applicable for the detec tion of discontinuities that are open 
to the surface in components of non-porous metallic material , welds and 
temporary attachments. 
 
 
2.0 SCOPE 
 
As specified in the particular Inspection Program of the component . 
 
 
3.0 CODES 
 
ASME Code, Section V – Non Destructive Examination (2017 Edition). 
ASME Code, Section VIII Div 1 y 2 – Pressure Vessels (2017 Edition). 
ASME Section I, Power Boilers (2017 Edition).

It will also be valid that the subcontractor will be certified according to their 
own written practice. If the subcontractor's written practice is appr oved by the

The results must be interpreted by a Level II or III qualified and certified 
personnel.

6.0 SURFACE CONDITION 
 
6.1.0. CLEANING 
The areas to be examined and all adjacent areas within at least 1 inch ( 25 
mm.) will be clean (free of dust , oxide, welding splatter, oils, machining marks 
or any other that can interfere with the exam or the interpret ation) and will be 
dry before applying the penetrant.

Drying after cleaning of the surface to be examined s hall be accomplished by

The temperature of the penetrant and the examined surface will be between 
the 5ºC and 52ºC throughout the examination period. Local heating or cooling 
is permitted. 
 
6.3.0 LIGHTING 
For visible penetrant, t he minimum light intensit y will be 1000 lux. The l ight 
intensity shall be measured with light meter prior to the evaluation of 
indications. 
 
Light meters shall be calibrated at least once a year or whenever the meter 
has been repaired. If meters have not been in use for one year or more, 
calibration shall be done before being used.

minimum and maximum listed in Table 1 (attached) 
 
The examined area must be fully cover ed with the penetrant. This application

7.2.0 PENETRANT DWELL TIME 
The appropriate time of penetration for the range of temperatures indicated on 
the paragraph 6.2.0 of this procedure is at least of 15 minutes. 
 
During this period the penetrant liquid shall not be allowed to dry and shall be

For temperatures from 5ºC to 10ºC, minimum time shall be 20 minutes. When

The maximum penetrant dwell time shal l not exceed of 30 minutes according

7.3.0 EXCESS PENETRANT REMOVAL 
 
- Organic solvent 
 
First the excess penetrant shall be removed by wiping with a clean, dry, lint -
free cloth or absorbent paper, repeating the operat ion until most traces of 
penetrant have been removed. Later the cleaning process continues with rags 
moistened with solvent until the examined area and the adjacent ones will be 
fully cleaned. 
 
It is not allowed to apply the solvent directly on the examined area during the 
cleaning previous to the developer application. 
 
 
7.4.0 DRYING AFTER EXCESS PENETRANT REMOVAL 
The examined surfaces must be totally dry, before applying the developer. 
 
The surface may be dried by normal evaporation, blotting, wiping or forced air. 
 
Drying time after removal of excess penetrant and prior to the application of 
the developer shall be held to a minimum of 5 minutes and a maximum of 30 
minutes. 
 
 
7.5.0 DEVELOP ING 
The developer shall be applied as soon as possible, after excess penet rant 
removal. Prior to applyi ng wet developer to the surface, the developer spray 
must be thoroughly agitated to ensure adequate mixture. 
 
The developer shall be applied on the examined surface getting a fine layer free of 
local accumulations. 
 
7.6.0. INTERPRETATION 
Developing time f or final interpretation begins as soon as wet developer 
coating is dry. 
 
The f inal interpretation shall be made between 10 and 60 minutes after the 
developing time has been completed. 
. 
If the surface to be examined is large enough to precl ude complete 
examination, the examination shall be performed in increments. 
 
The surface must be observed during the application of the developer to 
follow the behaviour of the indications and during the test.

8.0 EVALUATION 
 
An indication is the evidence of a mechanical discontinuity. 
An indication of an imperfection may be larger than the imperfection that 
causes it; however, the size of the indication is the basis for acceptance 
evaluation. Only indications with major dimensions greater than 1,5 mm shall 
be considered relevant. 
 
• Linear indications are those having a length exceeding three tim es the 
width of the indication. 
 
• Rounded indications are those which are circular or elliptical and have 
a length of less than three times the width of the indication. 
 
• Any doubtful or questionable indication will be checked again to verify if 
it is relevant. 
 
Any indication that is believed to be non-relevant shall be regarded as a defect 
until the indication is eliminated by surface conditioning or evaluat ed by other 
non-destructive means and demonstrated to be non-relevant. 
 
When two adjacent indications are separated by a lower distance than the 
bigger dimension of the smallest one, they should be evaluated as a single 
continuous indication.

6.2.0 TEMPERATURA 
 
La temperatura de la superficie examinada y de los productos deberá est ar entre 
los 5 ºC y 52 ºC durante el periodo de inspección. Calentamiento o enfriamiento

6.3.0 ILUMINACIÓN 
 
La mínima iluminación será de 1000 lux en la superficie a examinar. La intendidad 
de la luz deberá ser medida con un luxómetro pr evío a la evaluación de las 
indicaciones. 
 
El luxometro deberá ser calibrado como mínimo una vez al año o cada vez que se 
repare. Si el equipo no ha sido usado en un año o más, se deberá calibrar antes de 
su uso.

7.3.0 ELIMINACION DEL EXCESO DE PENETRANTE 
 
- Disolvente orgánico 
 
En un primer momento el exceso de penetrante se eliminará con papel absorbente 
o con trapos limpios, secos y libres de pelusa, repitiendo la operación hasta que se 
eliminen la mayoría de los restos de penetrante Posteriormente se seguirá 
limpiando con trapos humedecidos con disolvente frotando tanto la zona 
examinada como las adyacentes hasta que no se aprecie ningún resto de 
penetrante. 
 
No está permitido aplicar el eliminador directamente sobre la zona examinada 
durante la limpieza anterior al revelado. 
 
7.4.0 SECADO DESPUES DE ELIMINAR EL EXCESO DE PENETRANTE. 
 
Las superficies inspeccionadas deberán estar completamente secas, antes de 
aplicar el revelador. 
 
Las superficies se pueden secar por evaporación, (exposición a temperatura 
ambiente), secando con material absorbente o mediante soplado con aire. 
 
El tiempo de secado después de eliminar el exceso de penetrante y previo a la 
aplicación del revelado deberá ser entre un mínimo de 5 minutos y un máximo de 
30 minutos. 
 
 
7.5.0 REVELADO 
 
El revelador deberá ser aplicado lo antes posible tras la eliminació n del exceso de 
penetrante. Antes de aplicar el revelador húmedo a la superficie, el bote 
pulverizador deberá ser agitado para asegurar la mezcla adecuada. 
 
El revelador deberá aplicarse uniformemente sobre la superficie examinada 
consiguiendo una capa fina libre de acumulaciones locales.

después del secado del revelador húmedo. 
 
La interpretación final se hará entre 10 y 60 minutos después de que el tiempo de 
revelado haya transcurrido. 
 
Si la superficie a examinar es lo suficientemente l arga que imposibilita el examen 
completo, el examen se realizará en fases.

8.0 EVALUACION 
 
Una indicación es la evidencia de una imperfección mecánica. 
 
Una indicación de una imperfección podrá tener mayor longitud que la 
imperfección que lo causa, sin embargo los criterios de aceptación se basan 
principalmente en el tamaño de las indicaciones. Solo las indicaciones con 
dimensiones mayores a 1,5 mm se considerarán relevantes: 
 
• Indicaciones lineales son aquellas en las cuales la longitud es mayor que tres veces 
la anchura. 
 
• Indicaciones redondeadas son aquellas de forma circular o elíptica cuya longitud es 
menor que tres veces la anchura. 
 
• Cualquier indicación dudosa o cuestionable será comprobada nuevamente para 
verificar si es o no relevante.
