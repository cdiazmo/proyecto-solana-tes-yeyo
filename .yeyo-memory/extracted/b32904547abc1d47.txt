Subject: Executive Summary; Various Secondary Small to Medium Foundations
The following are the result of the partial special inspection for the various small to medium foundations.
Rebar and Forms Special Inspection, foundations, see list Table 1:
1) All critical dimensions where inspected and the installation of the steel cage was done per blue prints,
a. The forms as well as the cage meet the required applicable sections of the ACI318-14,
b. Special attention was given to the requirements for anchoring and cage interfaces,
i. There was a concern on water runoff, pumps were used to solve the issues,
c. The pre-pouring process was done without any delays, process was flawless, process completed
January 22, 2019.
Reinforced Concrete Special Inspection, Foundations, see list Table 1:
2) I witnessed the pouring of concrete, slump test was performed on site, all registered values were less than 8
inches, samples were taken for additional testing, by a third party representing Speedie and Associates (3331
East Wood Street, Phoenix Arizona 85040),
a. OSHA best practices were observed during the pouring of reinforced concrete,
b. Weather conditions were normal and values recorded, no high winds recorded,

i. Excessive runoff overnight caused some problems that were cured by using pumps and two
footings pouring were postponed due to excessive plastic clay near rebars in a localized
area, F18B and F26.

ii. Postponing pouring on two of the footings, F26 and F18B was done by the construction
team, in my opinion this happened due to overnight rain/runoff concentrating plastic clay
under the rebar cage this can potentially create spalling of the rebars, | agree with this
decision. Cage will have to be lifted and plastic clay in contact with rebar shall be removed.
In my opinion this happen thru an act of God. This will have an impact on the ordered
quantity of concrete.

c. The entire process went flawlessly, outside of the issues with F26 and F18B.
