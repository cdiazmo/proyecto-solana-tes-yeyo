1 Scope
1.1 This specification covers the materials and application requirements for externally coated carbon 
steel pipe and fittings for underground installations. The system must be suitable for use at 
150 F. (maximum). The minimum temperature requirement is 0 F.
1.2 The entire coating system shall be of one type, matching any previously applied coating system 
(new construction) and shall be applied following the manufacturers recommended methods 
and procedures.
1.3 All coated piping shall be inspected by the coat er (shop or field) by means of an approved 
electrical flow detector, delivering approximately 8,000 to 12,000 volts at low amps. Any defects 
shall be repaired and the repaired areas shall again be tested with the detector to make sure 
repairs are adequate.
1.4 All of the following coating systems are approved as equal, excepting temperature limitations 
and all general conditions shall be applicable for each individual coating system. At the time of 
quotation, the vendor must indicate which coating system is to be furnished.
2 General
2.1 Before the application of any coating system all associated pipe and fittings shall be cleaned per 
Steel Structure Painting Council specification SSPC-SP-6 “commercial blast cleaning”.
2.2 The pipe or fitting shall be left free of any pro tective substance 6” either side of any field joint 
location.
2.3 All coating shall be done in a neat, thorough and workmanlike manner. The coating system shall 
be applied in accordance with recommendations and instructions of the material manufacturer 
which shall form a part of this specification. All corner ridges, crevices and externally exposed 
surfaces must be coated.
2.4 All surfaces shall be free of dust, grease, oil and other foreign matter at the time any coating 
system is applied. No applications shall be done on damp surfaces, in freezing temperatures, 
when the steel temperature is below 40  F., when condensation is likely to occur or when the 
surface temperature of the steel is lower than 5 F. above the dew point of the surrounding air.
2.5 All prior coats shall be in good conditions at time of application of successive coats or wrapping. 
All bare or abraded spots shall be repaired.
2.6 Pipe and fittings that have been coated shall not be handled, worked on or otherwise disturbed 
until the coating is completely set.
2.7 Coated piping shall be handled with proper equipment (such as stout, wide fabric slings and 
wide padded skids) designed to prevent damage to the coating system. Bare cables, chains, 
hooks, metal bars or bare skids shall not be permitted to come in con tact with the coating 
system.

2.8 All field joints and damage repairs to any previously applied coating system shall be cleaned per 
Steel Structure Painting Council specification SSPC -SP-2 “hand tool cleaning ” and SSPC -SP-3 
“power tool cleaning ”. Surfaces con taminated with oil, etc., shall be cleaned per SSPC -SP-1 
“solvent cleaning”.
2.9 All pipe, fittings and welded joints shall be coated or coated and wrapped by one of the 
following systems as defined in section 3.0:
 The applied coating system.
 The applied coat and wrap system.
 The applied tape system
 The extruded polyethylene coating system.
3 Coating and wrapping systems
3.1 The applied coating system (-20 F. To +225 F.) Shall consist of one of the following items:
3.1.1 Carboline Company: a primer coat of Carbomastic #3 (6-8 mils when dry), a body coat 
of Carbomastic #14 (6 -8 mils when dry), and a seal coat of the same material and 
thickness as the body coat above, for a total minimum dry thickness of 18 mils.
3.1.2 Porter industrial coatings: a primer coat of MCR 4300 red pri mer (2-3 mils when dry), a 
body coat of C-200 coal tar epoxy (6-8 mils when dry), a seal coat of the same material 
and thickness as the body coat above for a total minimum dry thickness of 18 mils.
3.1.3 Abencs engineering department approved equal.
3.2 The applied coat and wrap system (-5 F. To +150 F.) shall consist of: (1) a coat of cold -applied 
coal-tar primer, (2) a coat of hot -applied coal-tar enamel into which is bonded a single layer of 
mineral felt wrap and (3) a final single wrapping of Kraft paper. The c oal-tar primer, coal -tar 
enamel and Kraft paper shall meet all requirements of American Water Works Association 
specification C203, latest issues. A mineral felt of comparable performance characteristics to that 
of felt covered under AWWA-S203, latest issue shall be utilized.
3.2.1 Immediately after cleaning, a single coat of coal -tar primer with a dry film thickness of 
1.5 to 2.0 mils shall be applied.
3.2.2 When the primer is dry, the primed surfaces shall be manually enameled by using the 
best grade of tampico fiber daubers. All surfaces shall be double coated by applying the 
enamel to a final dry film buildup of 3/32 ” and the allowable variation in enamel 
thickness shall not exceed  1/32”.
3.2.3 The mineral felt shall be spirally wrapped onto the hot-applied coal-tar enamel to form a 
definite and positive bond with the enamel. The lap of the felt shall be less then 1/2 ”. 
The felt shall be applied neatly and smoothly and shall be free of wrinkles and buckles.

3.2.4 Over the bonded mineral felt wrapper shall be applied a spiral w rapping of Kraft paper. 
The lap of the Kraft paper shall not be less than 1/2 ” and shall form a continuous 
protective coating.
3.3 The applied tape system (-20 F. to +150 F.) shall consist of: (1) coat of primer and (2) a layer of 
tape to which is bonded an outer protective wrapping.
3.3.1 The primer shall be TC primer -coat and the tape shall be “Tape-Coat 20 ” both as 
supplied by the Tapecoat Company.
3.3.2 Immediately after cleaning, a single coat of primer shall be applied by brush or rag at a 
rate of about 400 square feet per gallon to all clean external surfaces. The prime coat 
shall be applied to the surface immediately after heating the surface and while it 
remains warm per manufacturer’s recommendations. The prime coat shall dry to at least 
a tacky consistency before application of the protective coating.
3.3.3 After the prime coating has dried to a tacky consistency, the surface shall be spirally 
wrapped with tape. The coating side of the tape shall be heated with a wide mouth 
paint burning torch to obtain a softening or bleeding of the pitch. Only enough tape 
shall be heated that will remain in a softened state during application. The application 
shall proceed by alternately heating and spiral wrapping, with a minimum but thorough 
lap, until the entire surface is covered.
3.3.4 As the tape is unwrapped from the roll, the polyester film separator between the tape 
layers becomes the outer protective wrapping.
3.4 The extruded polyethylene coating system ( -20 F. to +140  F.) shall be per energy coating 
company “encoat” or Abencs engi neering department approved equal, consisting of: (1) a hot 
applied adhesive undercoating consisting of a blend of rubber, asphalt and high molecular -
weight resins, (2) an extruded plastic sheath consisting of prime virgin, high-density polyethylene 
copolymer.
3.4.1 The adhesive undercoating and extruded plastic sheath shall meet all requirements of 
federal specification L-C-530B (or latest issue). Type I coating system.
3.4.2 The minimum nominal plastic sheath thickness shall be as follows: pipe size 1/2 ” 
through 1 1/2” = 25 mils, pipe size 2 ” and 2 1/2 ” = 30 mils, pipe size 3 ” and 4” = 35 
mils, pipe size 5” through 24” = 40 mils.
3.4.3 The plastic sheath shall be identified with markings which comply with D.O.T. 
Regulations.
