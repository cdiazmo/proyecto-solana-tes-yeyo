1.0 OBJECT 
 
This procedure describes the method for the detection of open and subsurface 
discontinuities of ferromagnetic materials such as steel welds and vessel parts 
using the Yoke magnetizing method. 
The ferromagnetic particles can be used in medium wet or dry, and may be 
fluorescent or visible. It can be applied to the back gouged weld roots. 
 
 
2.0 SCOPE 
 
As specified in the I.P.P. or in the particular Inspection Program of the component.

5.0 EQUIPMENT 
 
 The equipment of magnetizat ion will be of AC yoke that produce longitudinal 
magnetisation. The yoke must have a pull force of 4,5 kg at the maximum pole 
spacing used. They should have adjust able pole, varying the magnetization 
distance between them from 3 to 6 inches (75 to 150 mm.) 
 
The magnetizing power of yokes shall be verified prior to use or whenever the 
yoke has been damaged or repaired 
 
 A UV-A lamp with a wavelength of 320- 380 nm and intensity not lower than 1000 
μW/cm
2 will be use with fluorescent particles. 
 
Light meters shall be calibrated at leas t once a year or whenever the meter has 
been repaired. If meters have not been in use for one year or more, calibration shall 
be done before being used.

7.0 DESCRIPTION OF THE PROCESS 
 
7.1.1 SUPERFICIAL CONDITION 
 
Before the magnetic particle examination, a check of the examination surface 
shall be conducted to locate any disc ontinuity surface opening which may not 
attract and hold magnetic particles because of their width. 
 
The weld to examine and adjacent areas within at least 25 mm, will be dry and 
clean, free of all dirt, grease, lint, scale, welding flux and spatter, husk, oxide, 
welding projections, oils, machining mar ks or any other strange materials that 
can interfere with the exam or the interpretation. 
 
 
7.1.2 MAGNETIC PARTICLE TECHNIQUES. 
The examination shall be done by the c ontinuous method. The method consists 
on the application of magnetic particles of very small size on the surface of the 
piece. 
 
The examination shall be performed as follows: 
 
DRY PARTICLES: 
 
The magnetizing current shall remain on while the examination medium is 
being applied and while any excess of the examination medium is removed.

8.0 EVALUATION 
 
An indication is the evidence of a mechanical imperfection. 
 
Only indications with major dimens ions greater than 1.5 mm shall be 
considered relevant. 
 
 Linear indications are those havi ng a length exceeding three times the 
width of the indication. 
 
 Rounded indications are those which ar e circular or elliptical and have a 
length of less than three times the width of the indication. 
 
 
 Any doubtful or questionable indication will be checked again to verify if 
it is relevant. 
 
When two adjacent indications are separ ated by a lower distance than the 
bigger dimension of the smallest one, they should be evaluated as a single 
continuous indication.

 Any cracks or linear indications
