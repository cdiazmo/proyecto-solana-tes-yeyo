1. Scope
This specification covers surface preparation and application of coatings and paint in the field as 
required. Items to be paint ed are shown on the attached drawings, schedules, or are listed in the body 
of this specification. 
2. General
2.1 Field assembly and required field tests for tanks, vessels and piping, necessary repairs, and 
re-testing where required shall be completed before starting final painting and touch-up work. 
2.2 Materials shall be delivered to the site in the paint manufacturer's sealed original containers 
bearing manufacturer's labels identifying the product and color. 
2.3 It shall be the Paint C ontractor's responsibility to provide his own storage facilities for materials. 
Storage may be in a building or trailer, but must meet all federal, state, l ocal and OSHA 
requirements and have all necessary permits. Purchaser will provide area for placement of 
storage. Storage sha ll maintain a constant temperature for storing materials in winter and 
summer according to paint manufacturer's instructions. 
2.4 Paint Contractor shall furnish all labor, power tools, scaffolds, ladders, liftin g, hoisting, 
compressors, spraying equipment, and all other appurtenances required for performance of the 
work. 
2.5 Failure to mention any governing codes in these specifications does not relieve the contractor 
from adhering to these codes in the scope of his work. 
2.6 The P urchaser shall resolve any confl icts between these specifications, paint manufacturer's 
instructions or any applicable codes identified by the contractor. 
2.7 Unless otherwise indicated, the following shall not be painted: Alloy 20, aluminum, lead, copper, 
stainless steel, galvanized steel, galvanized piping, plastics, cast iron and ductile iron. 
2.8 Painting operations, including placement of equipment, scaffolding, and supplies, are subject to 
direction of Construction Superintendent, to avoid interference with project operations and the 
work of other crafts, and to comply with OSHA's safety requirements. 
2.9 Drop-cloths shall be used to protect floors, walls, machinery, equipment, and previously painted 
surfaces as directed by the Construction Superintendent.

2.10 Do not apply paint in rain, snow, fog, or mist, or to any surface that may be wetted before paint 
has dried. No coating shall be applied unless surface temperature is a minimum of 5 oF above 
dew point. 
3. Surface Preparation
3.1 Surface preparation will be in accordance with the Steel Structu res Painting Council per 
individual paint specification. 
3.2 Surfaces that are not to be painted , such as sight glasses, name plates, identification numbers, 
instructions, flange faces, and valve gauges , shall be masked with durable materials that will 
protect against abrasive blast cleaning, paint, and ef fects of weather. When Feasible, Paint 
Contractor shall touch-up and seal nameplate edges after masking has been stripped. 
3.3 Sand Blasting
All sandblasting shall be done on a case -to-case basis so as not to interfere with work of other 
construction crafts. Paint Contractor must get approval from Construction Superintendent 
before starting any sandblasting. If necessary, sandblasting shall be done during off shift hours. 
Used blasting sand shall be removed from the work areas immediately after completion of 
surface preparation operations. 
3.3.1 Carbon Steel Surfaces - Materials used in abrasi ve blast cleaning shall be 16 -30 mesh 
sand, 40 -50 mesh steel grit. Particulate sizes larger or smaller than the ranges 
designated may be used with the permission of the Purchaser provided they produce the 
required profile depth. Surface preparation details will be per paint standard or 
specification.
3.3.2 Stainless Steel - Care must be taken to ensure mechanical cleaning method s do not 
contaminate stainless steel surfaces.
(1) Where sand blasting is applicable; (a) sandblast equipment must be cleaned 
before use to assure that all old iron contaminated sand has been removed, (b) 
sand and mineral grit must be iron and zinc free, and ( c) sandblast material 
must be new and not recovered/re-used.
(2) Cleaning brushes and tools used must not contain iron and they must not have 
been previously used on iron containing materials.

(3) Do not use chlorinated solvents on stainless steel surfaces. 
3.3 Uncoated Carbon Steel Surfaces
All steel surfaces that may not have any coating at all , including unpainted carbon steel pipes,
shall be prepared for painting per paint standard or specification. 
3.4 Pre-Painted Steel
Steel that has been coated in the shop with a prime and seal coat prior to delivery to the project 
site shall have abrasions, weld seams, and heat effected zone s cleaned for painting by wire 
brushing or other hand cleaning methods. 
3.5 Shop Primed Surfaces with Epoxy Primer
The specified epoxy primer must be seal coated within one year of application. Otherwise, the 
primer must be brush blasted to provide a proper surface profile prior to application of the seal 
coat. Unless otherwise indicated, the painter should assume a brush blast using fine (40 mesh) 
sand according to SSPC-SP 7 is required to get an adequate profile to insure adhesion. 
3.6 Shop Primed Surfaces with Inorganic Zinc Primer 
Surfaces that have been shop primed prior to delivery to the job site with inorganic zinc primer 
shall have voids, damaged and defective areas cleaned by wire brushing or other hand cleaning 
methods before applying the topcoat. If white powder (zinc oxide) substances are visible on the 
outside surface of the primer, most of it must be high -pressure water washed and scrubbed off 
the primer prior to application of the seal coat. There is no specified re-coat time for this paint.
3.7 Shop Seal Coated Surfaces with High Temperature Paint
There is no specified re-coat time for this paint. Painted surfaces remain soft until paint has been 
heat cured. Process heat is used for the curing process. Touch -up paint, prior to cure, can be 
applied without surface preparation. Cured paint surfaces must be brush blasted prior to 
application of touch-up paint.
3.8 Inter-Coat Contamination
Surfaces that become contaminated with deposits of any nature shall be cleaned by approved 
means before application of a succeeding coat of paint.

3.9 Mist Coat
When mist coat is requested over the seal coat, surfaces shall be prepared for painting by solvent 
cleaning, air blast, or brush blast as necessary to remove any visual contaminants. 
4. Application of Paint
4.1 Scheduling
4.1.1 Application of prime coat or finish coat shall be made on the same working day in 
which surface is prepared. 
4.1.2 Application shall be made with a minimum of delay after blast cleaning is completed. 
Blasted surfaces shall be painted on the same day before rust bloom occurs. If surfaces 
are allowed to become wet or to stand overnight before priming or finish coating, 
surface preparation will have to be repeated. 
4.1.3 Paint shall not be applied, when atmospheric and/or environmental conditions would (1) 
deter from the quality of the finished product, or (2) be injurious to adjacent property. 
The subcontractor shall install and m aintain a windsock or similar device to monitor 
wind direction. 
4.2 Touch-up Painting
Surfaces that have been painted in the shop with a self -priming paint or prime and seal coat 
prior to the delivery to the project site shall have welds, heat effected zones, abrasions and other 
damaged areas spot -cleaned per Section 3 of this specification. These areas shall be painted 
with the same paint as the original coat. The resulting dry film thickness shall be the same as the 
original coat. Refer to Section 6 o f this specification, the Pipeline Schedule, associated piping 
isometrics, and the Paint & Insulation Schedule for shop painted items.
4.3 Shop Primed Surfaces
Surfaces that have been primed in the shop shall be painted with the seal coat as specified in 
applicable paint standard. Shop primed surfaces shall be cleaned as specified under Section 3.5 
of this specification prior to the application of seal coat. Refer to Section 6 of this specification, 
the Pipeline Schedule, associated piping isometrics, and the Paint & Insulation Schedule for shop-
primed items.

4.4 Application Procedure
All painting procedures, workmanship, etc., shall be governed by the paint manufacturer's 
instructions, the applicable sections of Steel Structures Painting Council Spec. SSPC-PA-1-64, and 
this specification. 
4.4.1 Remove all residues from cleaned surfaces with a clean brush or with dry, oil -free 
compressed air. 
4.4.2 Each application of material shall be complete, full coverage coat that develops the 
specified dry film thickness as measured on uniform surface areas. Where "touch-up" is 
specified, complete coverage shall be achieved over the bare area, feathered well back 
over the sound paint remaining after the cleaning operation. 
4.4.3 Paint may be applied by spray apparatus, roller or b rush. Painting techniques that result 
in uniform, wet pattern shall be used. All coats shall be well worked into joints, corners, 
crevices, etc., and evenly distributed over flat surfaces. 
4.4.4 Each coat of paint shall be in a proper state of cure or dryness b efore the application of 
the succeeding coat unless otherwise directed in the paint manufacturer's printed 
instructions. 
4.4.5 Traffic and other work on painted surfaces must be prevented until the paint has cured.
5. Quality Control
5.1 Unless otherwise agreed, the purchaser or the inspector as his representative will observe work 
in progress and will inspect completed work for compliance with specified end conditions. 
5.2 Surface Preparation Quality 
5.2.1 Acceptability of surfaces prepared by hand cleaning or power tool c leaning will be 
judged on the basis of visual examination. 
5.2.2 The end condition of blast cleaning described in Section 3 will be judged by visual 
comparison with profile panels such as Clemtex, Ltd. surface profile gauges and 
cleanliness standards such as NACE Standard TM-01-70.

5.3 Painted Surfaces
5.3.1 Inspection of each co at of paint may be made at the P urchasers’ discretion. Film 
thickness and workmanship of the completed system will be examined. 
5.3.2 Film thickness specifications must be met by measurement of pl ane surfaces. Coverage 
of edges will be judged by visual examination. Thickness measurements on steel 
surfaces will be made with a calibrated Mikrotest gauge. Failure to meet specified 
thickness requirements is cause for rejection of the work. 
5.3.3 Work may also be rejected if there is evidence of inadequate drying or curing, or of poor 
workmanship. Acceptable finished work must be free of detrimental dust and dirt 
inclusions, abrasions, overspray, mud cracks, pinholes, runs, and sags, and must be 
uniform in color and even and continuous in appearance. 
5.4 Correction of Defects
5.4.1 The Contractor will be advised as quickly as possible of any defective or unacceptable 
work so that correction may be made with minimum delay. 
5.4.2 Non-conforming surface preparation and coated surfaces shall be corrected. 
5.5 Touch-up
A final touch -up will be required before leaving the job site by direction of the Construction 
Superintendent.
