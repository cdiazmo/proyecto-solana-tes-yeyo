1. Project Description 
 
Refer to Specification 4521-ESP-ANC-00-62-0001 Rev 2, Project Design Criteria 
 
2. Purpose 
 
This specification defines the scope of work, insul ating materials, and guidelines for the application of 
external insulation and weatherproofed protective c over systems onto piping, ducts, vessels and 
equipment in a solar plant for the purpose of conse rving heat, maintaining process temperatures and 
personnel protection. 
 
3. Performance 
 
 
3.1 Comments and exceptions to the specification shall be listed in a separate part of the design 
documents titled “Exceptions to the Specification”. All exceptions shall be listed in this section 
and shall reference the appropriate Specification section. No other exceptions will be allowed. 
 
3.2 The Contractor shall be responsible for all require ments delineated in this specification. All 
exceptions shall be noted in the Contractor’s desig n documents. If a purchase order is awarded, 
the Contractor shall be responsible to satisfy all requirements of this specification except for the 
agreed upon exceptions. 
 
3.3 The imperative language of this specification is di rected to the Insulation Subcontractor, unless 
specifically noted otherwise. Contractor is to per form all work described unless others are 
specifically identified as performing the work. Al l directives such as “provide”, “install”, 
“perform”, “test”, etc., are addressed solely to th e Contractor, whether or not the word 
“Contractor” is specifically mentioned. Work included for others will be specifically identified. 
 
3.4 Contractor shall be responsible for reviewing Pipin g and Instrument Diagrams (P&IDs), the 
vendor equipment drawing packages, and the insulati on requirements for each piece of 
equipment as needed to insure contractor’s familiar ity with equipment and installation 
requirements prior to submitting his bid.

and inspection of the insulation and protective, weatherproofed covering work. 
 
4.5 Contractor shall purchase and supply the specified insulation, protective coverings, and 
miscellaneous insulating / anchoring materials.

of hot insulation, weatherproofing, and overall appearance and long life.

7.4 General Application Guidelines 
 
 
7.4.1 Use pre-formed, molded, or shaped insulation segmen ts, as appropriate, when they are 
available. 
 
7.4.2 Insulation shall be applied in standard length sections, preferably 36” when possible. 
 
7.4.3 Insulation up to 3” thick may be applied in a singl e layer. Thicker insulation shall be 
applied in two (2) layers or more. 
 
7.4.4 All insulation shall be installed with staggered jo int construction, fit snugly, and have all 
edges tightly butted and held firmly together, with at least 2”overlap. 
 
7.4.5 All voids around gussets, manholes, nozzles, expans ion joints, etc., shall be firmly filled 
with loose insulation. 
 
7.4.6 Manway and access way covers shall be insulated sep arately. See Section 7.5. 
 
7.4.7 Provide expansion joints to accommodate differences in thermal expansion of the 
insulation and metal.

7.6 General Application Guidelines - Aluminum Jacketing 
 
7.6.1 Aluminum jackets shall be installed in a manner to shed water and shall have minimum 
overlaps of 3” for horizontal and circumferential seams. 
 
7.6.2 Where it is necessary to slit the aluminum jacket w hen forming around stiffeners, etc.,

and sealed so they are weather proof and will shed water. 
 
7.6.4 Jacketing shall be applied to the insulated work wh en it is in the cold condition (at 
ambient temperature).

to which they attach. Some of these clip brackets a re furnished attached to the item to 
be insulated and jacketed, some shall be provided a nd installed by the Insulation 
Subcontractor, as he requires. 
 
7.6.6 Inspection port and manhole cover jacketing shall b e removable and reusable. It shall be 
applied in such a manner that surrounding jacket is not damaged when the cover is 
removed, yet be weatherproof and shed water. 
 
7.6.7 Stainless steel screws with neoprene washers shall be used on longitudinal joints and for 
securing flashing sheets to provide watertight inst allations. Spacing of screws shall not 
exceed 9”.
