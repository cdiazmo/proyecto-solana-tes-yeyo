1.0 OBJECT 
 
This procedure describes the method fo r the detection of open and subsurface 
discontinuities in welds and vessel parts using the Yoke magnetizing method. 
The ferromagnetic particles can be used in medium wet or dry, and may be 
fluorescent or visible. It can be applied to the back gouged weld roots. 
 
 
2.0 SCOPE 
 
As specified in the I.P.P. or in the particular Inspection Program of the component. 
 
 
3.0 REFERENCES 
 
ASME Code, Section V – Non Destructive Examination (2017 Edition). 
ASME Code, Section VIII Div 1 y 2 – Pressure Vessels (2017 Edition). 
ASME Section I, Power Boilers (2017 Edition). 
ASME B 31.1 Power Piping (2016 Edition) 
WRITTEN PRACTICE ASME. QCM.PR05 
SNT-TC-1A (2006 Edition)

5.0 EQUIPMENT 
 
 The equipment of magnetizat ion will be of AC yoke that produce longitudinal 
magnetization. The yoke must have a pull force of 4,5 kg at the maximum pole 
spacing used. 
 
The magnetizing power of yoke s shall be verified prior to use or whenever the yoke 
has been damaged or repaired 
 
 A UV-A lamp with a wavelength of 320- 380 nm and intensity not lower than 1000 
μW/cm2 will be use with fluorescent particles. 
 
Light meters shall be calibrated at leas t once a year or whenever the meter has 
been repaired. If meters have not been in use for one year or more, calibration shall 
be done before being used.

6 .1 WET PARTICLES

6.2 DRY PARTICLES 
 
The operator must be sure that the powder used is adequat e for the temperature of 
the piece and that it is dry enough.

7.0 DESCRIPTION OF THE PROCESS 
 
7.1.1 SUPERFICIAL CONDITION 
 
Before the magnetic particle examination, a check of the examination surface 
shall be conducted to locate any disc ontinuity surface opening which may not 
attract and hold magnetic particles because of their width. 
 
The weld to examine and adjacent areas within at least 25 mm, will be dry and 
clean, free of all dirt, grease, lint, scale, welding flux and spatter, husk, oxide, 
welding projections, oils, machining mar ks or any other strange materials that 
can interfere with the exam or the interpretation. 
 
 
7.1.2 MAGNETIC PARTICLE TECHNIQUES. 
The examination shall be done by the c ontinuous method.The method consists 
on the application of magnetic particles of very small size on the surface of the 
piece. 
 
The examination shall be performed as follows: 
 
DRY PARTICLES: 
 
The magnetizing current shall remain on while the examination medium is 
being applied and while any excess of the examination medium is removed. 
Accumulations of excess dry particles in examinations shall be removed with 
a light air stream from a bulb or syringe or other source of low pressure dry 
air.

7.1.3. GENERAL REQUIREMENTS 
 
Field strength shall be che cked with a Berthol sample, having two lower cross 
section grooves. If two grooves are clearly seen, the magnetizing field shall be 
considered appropriate to perform the exam. 
 
The exam will be made applying at least two magnetic fields on the same area 
following the table 1 to guarantee the 100% exam of the surface. 
 
The applied magnetic field shall hav e sufficient strength to produce 
satisfactory indications, but shall not be so strong that it causes masking of 
relevant indications by non relevant accumulations of magnetic particles. 
Factors that influence t he required field strength include the size, shape and 
material permeability of part, etc.

8.0 EVALUATION 
 
An indication is the evidence of a mechanical imperfection. 
 
Only indications with major dimens ions greater than 1.5 mm shall be 
considered relevant. 
 
 Linear indications are those havi ng a length exceeding three times the 
width of the indication. 
 
 Rounded indications are those which ar e circular or elliptical and have a 
length of less than three times the width of the indication. 
 
 
 Any doubtful or questionable indication will be checked again to verify if 
it is relevant. 
 
When two adjacent indications are separ ated by a lower distance than the 
bigger dimension of the smallest one, they should be evaluated as a single 
continuous indication. 
 
 
 
9.0 ACCEPT / REJECT CRITERIA 
 
All surfaces to be examined shall be free of: 
 
According with ASME I, ASME VIII Div.1 & 2 
 
 Cracks like indications detected irres pective of surface conditions (ASME 
VIII Div 2 only) 
 
 Relevant linear indication. 
 
 Relevant rounded indications greater than 5 mm. 
 
 Four or more relevant rounded indicati ons in a line separated by 1,5 mm. or 
less (edge to edge). 
 
According with ASME B31.1 
 
 Any cracks or linear indications 
 
 
 Rounded indications greater than 5 mm 
 
 Four or more relevant rounded indicati ons in a line separat ed by 2 mm. or 
less (edge to edge). 
 
 Ten or more rounded indications in any 3870mm2 of surface with the major 
dimension of this area not to exceed 150mm with the area taken in the most 
unfavorable location relative to the indications being evaluated 
 
Only indications with major dimensions greater than 1,5 mm shall be considered 
relevant.

7.0 DESCRIPCION DEL PROCESO 
 
7.1.1 Condición superficial 
 
Previo al examen por partículas magnétic as, se debe realizar una inspección de la 
superficie con objeto de localiz ar cualquier indicación abierta a la superficie que 
pueda no atraer y mantener las partículas magnéticas debido a su anchura. 
 
La soldadura a examinar y las zonas ady acentes de al menos 25 mm, deben estar 
secas, limpias y libres de cualquier suciedad, aceite, pelusa, cascarilla, oxido, flux y 
proyecciones de soldadura, marcas de mec anizado o cualquier otra materia ajena 
que pueda afectar a la sensibilidad del ensayo. 
 
7.1.2 Técnica de partículas magnéticas 
 
La inspección se realizará conforme al método de campo continuo. El método 
consiste en la aplicación de partículas magnéticas de muy pequeño tamaño sobre 
la superficie de la pieza a examinar. 
 
El examen se realizará siguiendo el método descrito a continuación: 
 
PARTÍCULAS SECAS. 
 
La aplicación de las partículas se realiz ará mientras circula la corriente de 
magnetización sobre la piez a, la corriente de magnetización deberá permanecer 
mientras se elimina cual quier exceso de polvo. El exceso de partículas secas 
acumuladas en las inspecciones se eliminar á con una corriente de aire seco a baja 
presión.

8.0 EVALUACION 
 
Una indicación es la evidencia de una imperfección mecánica. 
 
Sólo se considerarán relevantes aquellas indicaciones cuyas dimensiones superen 
los 1.5 mm. 
 
 
 Indicaciones lineales son aquellas en las cuales la longitud es mayor que 
tres veces la anchura. 
 
 Indicaciones redondas son aquellas de forma circular o elíptica cuya 
longitud es menor que tres veces la anchura. 
 
 Cualquier indicación dudosa o cuestionable será comprobada nuevamente 
para verificar si es o no relevante. 
 
 
Cuando dos indicaciones adyacentes están s eparadas por una distancia inferior a 
la mayor dimensión de la más pequeña de las dos indicaciones deben tratarse 
como si fuera una sola indicación continua.
