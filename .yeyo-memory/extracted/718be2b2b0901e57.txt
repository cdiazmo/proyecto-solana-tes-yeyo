1 PURPOSE 
1.1 To define the internal procedure for hydraulic and mechanical performance test of centrifugal pumps and 
recording of the test results. 
 
2 SCOPE AND TEST CONDITIONS 
2.1 The tests will be carried out on the manufacturers test stand using cold water as test liquid.

and manufacturer and shall be fixed as a separat deviation list in written form.

5. TEST PROCEDURE 
To begin a performance test, the pumps must be fully machined and assembled. 
The liquid containing parts shall be subject to hydrostatic test prior to assembling.

identification. 
5.1.1 The pump tester shall read detailed order information for applicable information and shall verify that the 
pump is ready for test.

In General: 
 
The impellers will be balanced acc. to DIN ISO 1940 T1 in one or two planes on our rotational-balancing-
machine. The balancing in two planes will only be done if the ratio of the „impellers outer diameter“ and 
the „distance between the two compensation sections“ is smaller than 6. 
 
1. Balancing Machine 
 
Manufactuer : Company Schenk Ro Tec GmbH 
Type : ROTEC HM 30U 
Year of manufacture : 2001 
Machine-service and 
calibration 
: Annually by manufacturer 
 company Schenk 
 
 
 
 
2. Sequence 
 
2.1 Finalized impeller will be brought to the balancing machine. 
 
2.2 Mounting of the auxiliary shaft in its seat which will be used again for mounting the impeller. 
 
2.3 Adjusting of balancing machine, activating and obtaining the point(s) of imbalance of the impeller. 
 
2.4 Elimination of the imbalance through milling, grinding, drilling or building-up by welding of 
 material at the rotor. 
 
2.5 Obtaining of remaining imbalance and checking if the obtained value is inside of the 
 allowable limits according to DIN ISO 1940, Grade 2.5 – If „No“ then restarting from point 2.4. 
 
2.6 Signing of „working papers“ and release of the impeller. 
 
Example Impelle 
 
For all impellers 1-levels-balancing (single-plane), if D/B ≥ 6. 
For all impellers 2- levels-balancing (double-plane), if D/B < 6.

8.2 Applying of the penetrant 
The penetrant is applied by spraying or brushing. During th dwell time the penetrant must be liquid on all 
areas. A drying oft he penetrant is not allowed. 
8.3 Dwell time of penetrant 
The dwell time ranges from 10 to 30 minutes, depending on the surface condition and the material. As 
required the parts must be turned, to avoid a pooling of the penetrant. 
8.4 Surface temperature 
The environmental and surface temperature range is between 10 and 50 °C. 
8.5 Removing excess penetrant 
After the dwell time of the penetrant has lapsed, the test piece will be cleaned with a jet of water (not 
sharp, with max. 3 bar,temperature between 10 and 38°C) with a distance of min. 30 cm. Do not wash 
off the work piece with water. 
8.6 Drying 
Drying of the workpiece must be performed immediately after cleaning the penetrant from the surface 
with a clean lint free cloth at room temperature. 
8.7 Developing 
The developer will be applied with spraying a thin and smooth film on the workpiece. The metallic bright 
on the surface must still be seen after spraying. The spray can has to shaken during spraying to mix the 
fluid in the Spray Can. Is the layer thickness to big, all the tested parts must be cleaned and all tests has 
to be redone. 
8.8 Developer dwell time 
The dwell time of the developer starts immediately after drying and varies from 10 min to 30 min. Stand-
ards or other specifications can force special dwell times (e.g. > 30 min). 
 Inspection, Eye examination 9
With a color contrast penetrant, the developer forms a reasonably uniform thin white coating. 
Surface discontinuities are indicated by bleed-out of the penetrant which is normally a deep red color 
that stains the developer. Indications with a light pink color may indicate excessive cleaning. 
Inadequate cleaning may leave an excessive background making cleaning and interpretation difficult. 
A minimum light intensity of 1000 lx (100 fc) is required on the surface to be examined to ensure ade-
quate sensitivity during the examination and evaluation of indications, see ASME Sec. V, Art. 6, T-676.3 
(ISO 3059, 5.3: 500 lx).

Dimension checking: 
 
After completion of the final assembly and the associated identity test, all pumps undergo a 
dimension inspection. 
All main and connection dimensions are checked using calibrated hand measuring 
instruments, the latest general arrangement drawing (AZ) and the Technical Pump Data Sheet 
of the relevant order. 
 
 
 
Visual examination / Final inspection: 
 
Prior to dispatch, the pumps are checked for visual appearance, cle anliness, labelling 
(direction of rotation marking, type plate etc.), conformity of design according to the 
specifications, as well as for completeness (add- on parts, spare parts, accessories), using the 
Technical Pump Data Sheet and the picking list of the relevant order. 
A further functional check (rotating the top shaft by hand) is performed, to eliminate any 
possible run-up of the impeller. 
 
All the openings in the pressure chamber (flange) are fitted with weather -resistant protective 
caps, to protect against damage and fouling. 
 
The pumps are packed in such a way that damage, fouling, and negative atmospheric 
influences are avoided.
