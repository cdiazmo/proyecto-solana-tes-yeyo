1. Project Summary

1.1 The Project is a concentrated solar power electric generating faility to be constructed on an
approximately 1835-acre parcel of an existing 3000 acre tract inMaricopa County, Arizona,
approximately 80 miles southwest of Phoenix, and 20 miles wet of the town of Gila Bend near
Interstate 8 and Painted Rock Dam Road.
The Project is a 280 MW (gross) parabolic trough solar power pint with a net nominal electrical
output of 250 MW, and all necessary supporting infrastructure which make a fully operational
facility.
The facility design includes a thermal energy storage system wilprovide up to six hours of solar
dispatchability. The solar field is oversized by about 70% relativto the actual calculated solar
thermal requirements to ensure the plant will achieve summer m-peak capacity factors of
approximately 100% from solar energy alone.
The Project design will incorporate parabolic trough technologyto capture solar energy in a heat
transfer fluid (HTF). Steam will be generated in heat exchanges, i.e., solar steam generators
(SSGs). The steam produced in the SSGs will then be sent to tva steam turbines which will
produce electrical power which will be sold to Arizona Public Service (APS). When the solar field
collects more energy than is needed to satisfy the requirementsof the Power Purchase
Agreement (PPA) with the utility, the design of the HTF systemshall incorporate the means for
the excess solar energy collected by the HTF system to be directed to the Thermal Energy System
(TES), where it will transfer its energy to molten nitrate salt dumg daytime hours with cloud
cover or during night hours.
