1. Scope 
 
This specification covers surface preparation, work manship, and application of paint inside the vendor 's 
shop to carbon steel, low alloy steel and stainless steel fabrications shown on drawings and schedules or 
specified in the purchase order. 
 
2. Application Conditions 
 
2.1 Completion of Prior Work - Fabrication and required shop tests for tanks, vessels, equipment 
items, and piping sections or spools, including necessary repairs and re-testing when required, 
shall be completed before starting painting work. 
 
2.2 Environmental Conditions - Surface preparation and painting of fabricated items shall be done in 
an area free of dust and contaminants from other shop work. Surface temperature of items to 
be painted will comply with paint vendor's instructions for minimum application temperature 
and relative humidity. 
 
3. Materials 
 
All painting work shall follow the requirements of the paint manufacturer as described in their latest 
product bulletins. Conflicts, between those requirements and the requirements of Abencs paint 
specifications shall be brought to the attention of the Purchaser for resolution. 
 
3.1 Paint Materials 
 
Materials as specified in the standards or specific ations listed below shall be used without 
deviation, unless the Purchaser approves substitution of other materials in writing.

3.2 Surface Preparation Materials 
 
3.2.1 Carbon Steel - Materials used in abrasive blast cleaning shall be 16 - 30 mesh sand, 40 - 
50 mesh steel grit. Particulate sizes larger or smaller than the ranges designated may be 
used with the permission of the Purchaser provided they produce the required profile 
depth. Surface preparation details will be per paint standard or specification. 
 
3.2.2 Stainless Steel - Care must be taken to ensure mechanical cleaning methods do not 
contaminate stainless steel surfaces. 
 
a Where sand blasting is applicable; (a) sandblast equipment must be cleaned 
before use to assure that all old iron contaminated sand has been removed, (b) 
sand and mineral grit must be iron and zinc free, and (c) sandblast material 
must be new and not recovered/re-used. 
 
b Cleaning brushes and tools used must not contain iron and they must not have 
been previously used on iron containing materials. 
 
c Do not use chlorinated solvents on stainless steel surfaces. 
 
4. Procedures and Workmanship 
 
4.1 The paint vendor’s instructions and the applicable sections of Steel Structures Painting Council 
Specification SSPC-PA-1-64 shall govern shop painting procedures and workmanship. 
 
4.2 Shop painting shall consist of the proper number of coats of paint material as noted in 
Paragraph 3. Method of application and dry film thickness (DFT) of each coat (in mils) shall be as 
shown in standards or specifications. 
 
4.3 The following surfaces shall not be painted: sight glasses and gauges, equipment identification 
nameplates, valve stems, plastic, rubber, copper, aluminum, galvanized steel, pre-finished metal 
furniture, glass and ceramic surfaces.

4.4 Masking 
 
4.4.1 Machined surfaces of fabricated items shall be masked with durable material that will 
protect the surface against abrasive blast cleaning, paint, and contamination. Unless 
other shipping protection is specified, masking shall remain during shipment. 
 
4.4.2 Mask cleaned flange faces and cleaned pipe lengths, and structural members with a 
durable material, one inch from each end or edge. Masking shall remain during 
shipment. 
 
4.5 Surface Preparation 
 
4.5.1 Fabricated Items - Surfaces of all uncoated fabricated items shall be prepared for 
painting as specified on paint system standards or specifications (refer to Par. 3.1 for 
standards to be used). Blasted surfaces shall be painted on the same day before rust 
bloom occurs. 
 
4.5.2 Placement of Work - Fabricated items to be painted shall be supported to minimize 
contact with the painted surfaces. Freshly blast-cleaned steel shall be supported on 
clean supports and when painted shall be high enough to eliminate the possibility of 
abrasive being blown into the paint. Dirty bucks shall be covered with polyethylene or 
other clean, suitable material. Vessels may be supported on unpainted leg or lug 
surfaces. 
 
4.5.3 Application of Paint - Follow the paint vendor's instructions for mixing, thinning, pot-
life, application, curing, and recoating. Remove all residues from cleaned surfaces with 
a clean brush or with dry, oil free compressed air.

4.6 Quality Control 
 
4.6.1 Unless otherwise agreed, the Purchaser, or the inspector as his representative, will 
observe work in progress and will inspect completed work for compliance with specified 
end conditions. 
 
4.6.2 The end condition of blast cleaning described in Par. 4.5 will be judged by visual 
comparison with profile panels such as Clemtex, Ltd. surface profile gauges and 
cleanliness standards such as NACE Standard TM 01-70. 
 
4.7 Painted Surfaces 
 
4.7.1 Measurement of paint dry film thickness will be made with a calibrated Mikrotest 
gauge. Failure to meet specified thickness requirements is cause for rejection of the 
work. 
 
4.7.2 Work may be rejected if there is evidence of inadequate drying or curing, or of poor 
workmanship. Acceptable finished work must be free of detrimental dust and dirt 
inclusions, abrasions, over-spray, mud cracks, pinholes, runs, and sags, and must be 
uniform in color and even and continuous in appearance.

4.8 Correction of Defects 
 
4.8.1 The Supplier will be advised as quickly as possible of any defective or unacceptable work 
so that corrections can be made with a minimum of delay. 
 
4.8.2 Inadequate surface preparation and rejected portions of the paint shall be corrected as 
agreed upon by the Purchaser and the Supplier. 
 
4.9 Handling of Painted Items 
 
4.9.1 Painted steel shall not be handled or moved until paint is sufficiently dry and cured to 
minimize abrasion and scarring. 
4.9.2 Steel cables or chains are not to be used for lifting or loading. All handling, lifting and 
loading shall be done with belt slings. 
 
4.9.3 Painted steel loaded for shipment shall be protected from direct steel-to-steel contact 
with wood or other suitable spacers. 
 
4.9.4 Cable, strap, or chain used to tie-down and secure steel loaded for shipment is 
separated from painted steel surfaces suitable wood or rubber spacers or mats. 
 
4.10 Inspection 
 
Purchaser has the authority and privilege of inspection of all work to assure conformance with 
specifications. The supplier responsible for surface preparation and painting shall notify 
Purchaser one week prior to the date the material will be ready for painting.
