1. Project Summary 
 
1.1 The Project is a concentrated solar power elect ric generating facility to be constructed on an 
approximately 1835-acre parcel of an existing 3000 acre tract in Maricopa County, Arizona, 
approximately 80 miles southwest of Phoenix, and 20 miles west of the town of Gila Bend near 
Interstate 8 and Painted Rock Dam Road. 
 
The Project is a 280 MW (gross) parabolic trough solar power plant with a net nominal electrical 
output of 250 MW, and all necessary supporting infrastructure, which make a fully operational 
facility. 
 
The facility design includes a thermal energy storage system will provide up to six hours of solar 
dispatchability. The solar field is oversized by about 70% relative to the actual calculated solar 
thermal requirements to ensure the plant will achieve summer on-peak capacity factors of 
approximately 100% from solar energy alone. 
 
The Project design will incorporate parabolic trough technology to capture solar energy in a heat 
transfer fluid (HTF). Steam will be generated in heat exchangers, i.e., solar steam generators 
(SSGs). The steam produced in the SSGs will then be sent to two steam turbines which will 
produce electrical power which will be sold to Arizona Public Service (APS). When the solar field 
collects more energy than is needed to satisfy the requirements of the Power Purchase 
Agreement (PPA) with the utility, the design of the HTF system shall incorporate the means for 
the excess solar energy collected by the HTF system to be directed to the Thermal Energy System 
(TES), where it will transfer its energy to molten nitrate salt during daytime hours with cloud 
cover or during night hours.

4.1.2 All field welds (FW) for HTF service piping shall r eceive 100% radiographic examination. 
Since socketweld joints cannot be radiographed, all socket weld connections shall be 
examined by visual in-process examination.

examined will be selected by an Abener Teyma repres entative. Welds will be examined 
using Ultrasonic Testing (UT). UT testing procedur e will employ both Shearwave and 
Phased Array Ultrasonic Test modes. Any weld defec ts found will be followed by 
additional progressive sampling and examinations per B 31.3, paragraph 341.3.4. 
 
Each weld tested shall have the following documentation: 
 
1) Table of welds tested with pass/fail summary 
2) Cut plan view field sketch of each weld tested indi cating defects, flaws and 
clean/passed weld portions 
3) Ultrasonic data printout generated by UT unit showi ng defect indication and 
machine parameters 
4) Reviewable electronic file back up of weld scan UT data

5. Special Considerations 
 
5.1 Fabrication Tolerances 
 
5.1.1 Scope: This specification shows tolerances for end preparation, dimensioning and 
component alignment in the fabrication of metallic piping. 
 
5.1.2 Reference Standards: The latest approved editions of the following standards apply, 
except as modified herein: Factory-Made Wrought St eel Building Fittings - ASME B16.9; 
Pipe Flanges and Flanged Fittings - ASME B16.5; ASM E B16.47 Series A, only. 
Fabricating Tolerances - PFI ES-3. (PFI - Pipe Fabrication Institute, Springdale, PA) 
 
5.1.3 Tolerances: 
 
(1) End Preparation: Pipe, fitting and valve ends shall be prepared for welding as 
specified in the welding procedure specification. Preferred practice is shown in 
Figure 1. 
 
(2) Linear Dimensions: Tolerances shown in Figure 2 ap ply to face-to-face, face-to-
end and end-to-end measurements of fabricated pipin g spools and to center-
to-center, center-to-face and center-to-end measure ments of piping offsets, 
bands, nozzles and support attachments. 
 
(3) Angular Tolerances: The included angle of a pipe b end shall not deviate more 
than +0.5 degree from that specified. 
 
(4) Exceptions: Closer tolerances shown on drawings supersede this specification.
